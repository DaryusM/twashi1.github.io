WhatsApp

WhatsApp is a free messaging service that started in 2009 as an alternative to SMS.

Main Features

  1. Text Messaging: Send and receive text messages in real-time.
  2. Voice Calls: Make voice calls to other WhatsApp users over an internet connection.
  3. Video Calls: Conduct video calls for face-to-face communication.
  4. Group Chats: Create and participate in group chats with multiple contacts.
  5. End-to-End Encryption: WhatsApp provides end-to-end encryption for messages, ensuring privacy and security.
  6. Media Sharing: Share photos, videos, documents, and audio files with contacts.
  7. Status Updates: Post short-lived status updates that disappear after 24 hours.
  8. WhatsApp Web: Use WhatsApp on a web browser to chat from a computer.

End-to-End Encryption

Since the start of the century as SMS texting began getting more popular, there have been multiple reports of texts being intercepted and read by the government ( Channel 4 Spy Report ).
WhatsApp uses end-to-end encryption to ensure that only the intended recipients can read the messages you send.

  1. Key Exchange: When you first set up WhatsApp, the app generates a pair of encryption keys â€“ a public key and a private key. The public key is shared with your contacts, while the private key remains on your device.
  2. Signal Protocol: WhatsApp employs the Signal Protocol, a highly regarded encryption protocol, to encrypt messages and calls. Each message or call is protected with a unique session key.
  3. Secure Transmission: When you send a message, your device encrypts it using the recipient's public key. Only the recipient's device, with the corresponding private key, can decrypt and read the message. This ensures that even WhatsApp itself cannot access the content of your messages.
  4. Forward Secrecy: WhatsApp employs forward secrecy, which means that even if someone were to obtain your encryption keys in the future, they cannot decrypt past messages, as each message is encrypted with a unique session key.
  5. Authentication: Messages are not only encrypted but also authenticated to prevent tampering or spoofing. This ensures that the sender and content remain secure during transmission.
  6. Group Chats: Group chats in WhatsApp also benefit from end-to-end encryption. Each group has a shared encryption key, and individual messages within the group are encrypted and decrypted using this key.

WhatsApp is not the only application that uses end-to-end encryption, but it is one of the best examples of how computational thinking and computational power has been used to bring a service to the masses that allows for private communication.
